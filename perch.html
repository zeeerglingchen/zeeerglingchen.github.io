Design Process

Understanding

User Interview

I conducted 5 interviews with 5 PhDs from University of Michigan and wanted to understand their goals and frustrations and evaluate the current website. The interview consists of two parts: (1) General job searching experience and (2) their experience of using the website. I found out three samiliar pain points during interviews:

Competitive Analysis

With the insights from user interviews, I conducted a competitive analysis to help me learn from currently existing solutions in the market. I also realized how Perch is unique as a job searching platform for researchers. Athough big companies like Indeed and LinkedIn also provide some relevant jobs for researchers, here is still room for them to grow to meet needs of researchers and solve their pain points.

One insight I got from the analysis is that, unlike Perch users have to log in to view job postings, LinkedIn and Indeed allow users to explore most content on the website without logging in. Visitors will be asked to create an account when they want to do something on the platform.I believe this user flow is better than what we currently have.

Prototyping & Key Features



1. CV to Resume Conversion

Users can convert their long and detailed CV to a concise and customized resume with one click. To begin with, users need to upload their CV onto the website which would parse the CV to get all the information.

Users can either choose the content template provided by the website or select the section they want to include in the resume.

In the meantime, the website can gain detailed information about the user and provides customized service to users.

2. Video Resume

One insight we got from user interviews with recruiters is that they are concerned about the communication skills of Ph.D. students. Although this understanding is a little bit biased, we consider this as a good opportunity for Ph.D. students and researchers to show their social skills by implementing the video resume feature.

The video resume normally would be one minute long. Users can use guideline we provide during the recording to organize their thoughts and be well-prepared.

3. Job & Faculty Searching Results

The previous searching result page is two-column layout. To improve the efficiency of viewing all the job or faculty information for uses, I adapted the three-column layout to show the filter, the job list and detailed information of one job on the same page.

It is much easier for users to check detailed information of different jobs on one page rather than jumping back and forth among different tabs.

4. Convert Visitors to Users

Perch welcomes new visitors. Actually, we allow people to submit job applications without having a Perch account. But there are certain key features that only logged-in users can use.

We encourage visitors to sign up and complete their profile by showing them those features.

5. Multiple Ways to Validate Skills

The skill validation is a pain point for both job seekers and posters, especially for Ph.D. students who have many highly-specialized skills. We provide 2 ways for users to validate their skills, which are validation by uploading a certificate and by validation from a faculty.

6. Easy and Skippable Onboarding

I reduced the onboarding questions from 10 to 4 and all of them are skippable. I want users to access the main content on the website as quick and easy as possible and collect their information when they have to provide such information to achieve their goals like uploading a resume to apply to a job.

7. Dashboard

Users can track their applications progress and view all their saved jobs as well as job recommendations in the dashboard.
